The Science

Built on behavioral science.
Not opinion.

01
Systematic Desensitisation
By repeatedly exposing your dog to departure triggers without the departure, the predicted threat response extinguishes. The amygdala stops firing. The anxiety response stops.
02
Counter-Conditioning
When "keys = nothing happens" replaces "keys = owner leaves," the neurological prediction changes permanently. This is neuroplasticity — it takes repetition but the gains are lasting.
03
Threshold Management
Your dog must never experience a full panic attack during training. One flooding event sets back weeks of progress. PAXA Solo keeps every session sub-threshold — always.

Questions

Answered honestly.

Is this suitable for severe separation anxiety?
PAXA Solo is built for mild to moderate separation anxiety. If your dog scores 6–10 on the Anxiety Scorecard, we recommend consulting a veterinary behaviourist alongside the protocol. Medication can support faster progress in severe cases.
How much time does it take each day?
Training is capped at 30 minutes per day by design. Overtraining is as harmful as under-training. The nervous system needs recovery time to consolidate learning.
My dog has had anxiety for years. Will this still work?
Learned responses can be unlearned — regardless of how long they've been present. The neurological mechanisms that create separation anxiety are the same ones that reverse it. Duration doesn't determine outcome. Consistency does.
Do I need any special equipment?
A camera (your phone works perfectly), a mat or blanket for your dog's place, and optionally a remote treat dispenser for Phase 1. All of this is explained in detail in the workbook.
What if it doesn't work for my dog?
