## Voice & Tone — 4 Pillars

### 1. Calm Authority
We speak with the quiet confidence of someone who has solved this problem before. No hype. No urgency theatre. No countdown timers. We state what's true and let it land.

> *"Separation anxiety isn't a personality trait. It's a learned response. And learned responses can be unlearned."*

### 2. Empathetic Precision
We acknowledge the emotional reality of the owner — the guilt, the helplessness, the 4pm camera-check spiral — and then we move them forward. We sit with the feeling for one sentence, then we give them something to do.

> *"You didn't cause this. But you can fix it."*

### 3. Counter-Intuitive Confidence
PAXA earns trust by saying things that sound wrong but are right. We use behavioural science as our permission to contradict conventional pet wisdom. This surprises, educates, and builds authority simultaneously.

> *"The worst thing you can do when you get home is comfort your dog."*

### 4. Dignified Directness
No waffle. No filler. Every sentence earns its place. We respect the reader's intelligence and their time.

> *"The protocol works. The question is whether you follow it."*

## UK vs AU Copy Register

**UK (Phase 1):** Drier. More self-aware. Slightly ironic. Premium without being pompous. Think: a very good vet who also has a dry sense of humour.

**AU (Phase 2):** Warmer. More direct. Less formal. Same science, more approachable delivery.

---

## Writing Rules — Enforced

1. UK English spelling always (behaviour, colour, recognise, practise)
2. No exclamation marks in product or brand copy
3. Em dashes (—) not hyphens for pauses
4. Numbers under 10 written as words in body copy
5. Italic used for emphasis, never bold in body copy
6. Sentences start the paragraph — never bury the lead
7. Science terms used correctly: systematic desensitisation, counter-conditioning, threshold, cortisol, amygdala, neuroplasticity
8. Product always called "PAXA Solo" — never "the workbook" alone
