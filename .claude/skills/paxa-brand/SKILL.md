# PAXA Brand Context

## What PAXA Is
Premium UK digital pet wellness brand. Core product: PAXA Solo — a 30-day behavioral protocol PDF (£29) that solves dog separation anxiety. Target market: UK dog owners, age 28–55, educated, skeptical of gimmicks.

- Landing page: https://paxapet.co.uk

- Email: hello@paxapet.co.uk
- Price: £29 one-time, instant download

## Brand Voice — The Rules

### Always
- Science-backed, calm authority
- UK English spelling (behaviour, desensitisation, recognise)
- Counter-intuitive hooks ("The worst thing you can do when you leave your dog...")
- Specific numbers and science references (cortisol, amygdala, neuroplasticity)
- Empathetic but precise — acknowledge the problem, then move to solution
- Premium, dignified tone for both owner and dog

### Never
- "Fur baby", "pawesome", "puppy parent", or any cutesy portmanteau
- Exclamation marks in product copy
- Countdown timers, urgency theatre, "only X spots left"
- "Revolutionary", "guaranteed overnight results"
- "Join thousands of happy pet parents"

## Core Product Messaging
- PAXA Solo is a behavioral PROTOCOL, not a calming supplement
- It TEACHES the dog to be calm — it does not mask symptoms
- Built on: systematic desensitisation, counter-conditioning, functional relaxation
- 4 phases: Foundation (Days 1–7), Desensitisation (8–14), Incremental Distance (15–21), Real-World Resilience (22–30)
- Key science fact: cortisol peaks at 30–40 minutes — the "40-minute window"
- Key insight: "Boring goodbyes save lives" / "The worst thing you can do when you get home is comfort your dog"

## Key Taglines
- Primary: "Peace for your pet. Science for you."
- Product-led: "30 days. One protocol. A different dog."
- Challenger: "Every other brand treats the symptoms. We treat the cause."

## Target Customer
UK dog owner, 28–55, has a velcro or anxious dog, has tried calming treats and YouTube videos that didn't work, educated enough to respond to science framing, willing to pay £29 for something that actually works.

## Competitors & Differentiation
PAXA is NOT competing with supplements, anxiety wraps, or vet consultations. It is competing with the idea that "there's nothing you can do" or "just get a second dog." PAXA's positioning is: this is a neurological training problem, and it has a structured solution.
